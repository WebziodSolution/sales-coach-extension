Why Do Anything:
1. What’s driving the urgency to solve this now?
2. How is this issue impacting the business today?
3. What happens if this problem is not addressed?

Business Value:
4. If this were solved, what measurable business improvement would you expect?
5. How is this impacting revenue, productivity, time, or cost today?
6. What key business metrics are you hoping to improve?
7. What business outcomes matter most to the executive sponsor?

Economic Buyer:
8. Who ultimately approves initiatives like this?

Champion:
9. Who is most motivated to solve this problem?
10. Who would champion this internally?

Decision Criteria:
11. What business or technical requirements are most important in selecting a solution?

Decision Process:
12. If we mapped out the path to a final decision, what steps would need to happen and who would be involved?

Paper Process:
13. What internal procurement, legal, or security steps should we plan for?
14. What typically slows down or delays the purchasing process?

Current Environment:
15. What solutions or processes are in place today?
16. What other solutions are being considered?
17. What concerns or risks do you see with moving forward?

Next Steps:
18. What would make sense as a next step from here?
19. What additional information would be helpful for your team?
20. Who should be involved in the next conversation?
